Modding Helper Documentation

Author: Vanity8

This tool's snap detection is based on beatLength and does not consider meter (time signature). Therefore, 1/1 snap means "the position of each beat," not the beginning of the measure.

Clap / Whistle

Counts Whistle / Clap / Whistle + Clap for Circle objects only (type=1/5).

When the checkboxes are enabled, the corresponding timestamps are also displayed.

1ms Offset

Detects HitObjects that are shifted by ±1–3 ms from their expected snap positions.

Snap positions are calculated from the .osu file’s beatLength, and decimal values are truncated.

The snap values checked by this tool are: 1/1, 1/2, 1/3, 1/4, 1/5, 1/6, 1/7, 1/8, 1/9, 1/12, 1/16. Other snap values are not detected.

Offsets of ±1 ms are not detected by Mapset Verifier or AiMod.
However, ±1 ms offsets are generally rankable.

Kiai Compare

Compares the total kiai time between difficulties.

If the total kiai duration differs, the tool shows the exact sections where the kiai state does not match.
It also warns when the end point of kiai time is not explicitly set.

Mapset Verifier can compare total kiai duration, but it cannot show the exact mismatching sections.

Kiai Snap

Checks whether kiai ON/OFF timing points are placed on proper snap positions.

In addition to unsnapped kiai lines, this tool detects misaligned snaps such as 1/8 snap.

AiMod can detect unsnapped timing points, but it cannot detect these snap-based issues.

Double SV

Detects SV lines that are too close to each other within the selected threshold.

AiMod can detect SV lines stacked at the same timestamp, but it cannot detect double SV lines that are shifted by 1 ms.

SV Volume

Checks whether SV lines with volume changes are placed early enough before HitObjects.

If a volume-changing SV line is placed at the exact same timestamp as a HitObject, the new volume may not be applied when the player hits the note 1 ms early.

This check is not available in AiMod or Mapset Verifier.

Red / Green Match

Compares red lines (BPM lines) and green lines (SV lines) at the same timestamp.

  • volume mismatches
  • kiai ON/OFF mismatches
  • sampleset mismatches

The first red line cannot have kiai enabled. Therefore, if the first SV line has kiai enabled, it will be flagged by this check.

Also note that users may intentionally change volume at the first red/green line. Before that timing point, the red line volume is applied, and after that point, the green line volume is applied.

This check is not available in AiMod or Mapset Verifier.

SampleSet

TimingPoints

For red and green lines, the following are detected:

  • sampleset ≠ 1 (normal) (2: Soft / 3: Drum)
  • sampleIndex ≠ 0 → custom hitsounds

HitObjects

For notes, sliders, and spinners, the following are detected:

  • normalSet > 1 (2: Soft / 3: Drum)
  • additionSet > 1 (2: Soft / 3: Drum)
  • sampleIndex ≠ 0 → custom hitsounds
  • filename is specified → custom audio file

Hitsounds assigned to slider edges are also detected:

  • edgeSets.normalSet > 1 → 2: Soft / 3: Drum
  • edgeSets.additionSet > 1 → 2: Soft / 3: Drum

This check is not available in AiMod or Mapset Verifier.
